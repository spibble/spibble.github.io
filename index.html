what i'm up to rn

the date is 6/29/2026 (a bit late, but happy pride month!). i am currently working as a software/quality engineering intern at Brain Corporation, a commercial robotics company. they make stuff like autonomous floor scrubbers and shelf scanners :) the robots are pretty cute :) especially the smaller ones :)

this feels like the first "real" internship i've had before and the experience feels weird to adapt to, but it's been fun so far. it's fun getting to meet people in the office, eating free lunch, walking and talking, etc.; also, writing code is kinda fun, i guess. it's hard to say much because i started less than 2 full weeks ago, but so far, it's cool and i am learning a lot! it's also given me an excuse to stay in San Diego for the summer, which is nice because

  • i've never lived here in the summer,
  • i still have some friends to hang out with, and
  • i've never really made free time to go out and explore!

surprisingly, despite the time i've spent in SD, i never really got out that much. i've already visited a few new places in the past few weeks i've been off of school, particularly in the downtown area. i've also met up with some new people as well from other schools!

speaking of schools, i recently finished my third year at UCSD. academically, i found this year to be pretty challenging. i took (too) many classes this year, including my first graduate-level classes! some of my favorites from the past year are (in no particular order):

  • COGS 118B: Unsupervised Machine Learning. an introduction to things like clustering, expectation maximation, PCA, ICA, etc. the final project was anything ML-related of our choice, so our group did a difficulty classifier for Taiko no Tatsujin charts. i definitely wanna expand on this in the future!
  • COGS 189: Brain Computer Interfaces. an introduction to different types of brain signals, filtering, analysis methods, and some hands-on experience with EEG. for our final project we hooked up an EEG to keyboard inputs and mapped some controls in Minecraft :)
  • CSE 122: Wireless Networking. programming with Nordic/ESP boards to implement protocols like WiFi, Bluetooth, Thread, and Lora. the final project was a design project for a potential IoT application with hundreds of edge devices, which was cool.
  • CSE 200: Complexity Theory. an introduction to higher complexity classes past P/NP, like circuits, randomized algorithms, and interactive proofs. my brain still hurts from it but i survived and i thought it was pretty interesting!
  • CSE 276B: Human-Robot Interaction. a paper-based class for the HRI field, so we basically just read a bunch of papers about different robots and the effects they have on people. final project was a robot of our choosing so we made a robot that helps students study :)
  • MUS 177: Music Programming. programming oscillators, filters, etc. in C++. the class covered both software (DAW plugins) and hardware (using Electrosmith boards), but the project was only software. i made a horrible bitcrushing and delay plugin which you can find here!

despite all the fun i've had in school, it still gives me some stress. i'm currently in the process of applying for my master's in CS, which is never really fun, and i'm a little worried about job stability during the school year since a lot of scary budget cuts have been happening. but, at the end of the day, i'm sure thigns will be alright, and i will find my way :) i hope...

outside of school, i've just been chilling and developing my hobbies more. i've started playing more rhythm games more often (rhythm heaven groove in 3 days!!!) and i have a pretty diverse routine at Round1 now. i've found that being bad at a lot of games is generally more enjoyable than just being good at a couple (wait, isn't that kinda the same thing?). i've also found that i can connect with more people just by virtue of playing more games. (i'm also now the president of our school's rhythm game club, so i kinda feel like i have to play a lot of different games, lol)

i've also been spending more time furrying it up; since my first con experience at AFC last September, i've been looking into more and more cons! i went to FC back in January (highly recommend) and actually will be in PA this weekend for Anthrocon (if i met you there and you are reading this now, hello)! it's still kind of crazy to me that i'm doing all this just for a furcon, but hey, i need some hobbies to dump money into. this will be my first time traveling in a few years, so i'm looking forward to it!

other than that, i've pretty much just been chilling, thriving, in my lane. but perhaps i'll move out of my lane soon?
